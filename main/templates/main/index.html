According to the official CEFR guidelines, someone at the A1-level in English:
1 - Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed at the satisfaction of needs of a concrete type.
3 - Can introduce herself and others and can ask and answer questions about personal details such as where she lives, people she knows, and things she has.
3 - Can interact with other people in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help.

What can an A2-level learner of English do?
1 - Can understand sentences and common expressions about familiar topics, including very basic personal and family information, shopping, places of interest and work.
2 - Can communicate in simple, everyday tasks that require only simple and direct exchanges of information on familiar topics.
3 - Can describe aspects of their past, environment and matters related to their immediate needs, using simple language.

The CEFRL specifies for B1-level:
1 - Can cope with most of the situations that might arise on a trip to areas where the language is used.
2 - Is able to produce simple, coherent texts about topics with which they are familiar or in which they have a personal interest.
3 - Can describe experiences, events, wishes and aspirations, as well as briefly justifying opinions or explaining plans.

B2-level competencies:
1 - Can understand the main ideas of complex texts on concrete or abstract topics, including some technical discussions. 2 - Can express themselves fluently and spontaneously enough to comfortably communicate with other English speakers. 3 - Can produce clear, detailed text on many subjects and explain a complex viewpoint on a topic, including expressing advantages and disadvantages.

Skills at C1-level:
1 - Can understand a wide range of more difficult, longer texts and recognise implicit meanings.
2 - Can express themselves fluently and spontaneously with little need to search for the right expression.
3 - Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes. They can produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects.
